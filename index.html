About

I started as an XR developer — building VR training systems, scoping enterprise deployments, and sitting in enough customer discovery sessions to realize I was more interested in the sales problem than the build problem.

So I moved into Sales Engineering. Seven years later I've run 400+ discovery-led demos across mid-market and enterprise, supported deals from $25K to $150K ARR, and built internal workflows that cut pre-demo setup time by 50%. My background is Mechanical Engineering and Computer Science, which means I'm comfortable going deep on the technical side when I need to.

These days I spend extra time building the AI tools I wish I'd had as an SE — things that automate the repetitive, surface better signals, and save time without adding complexity. This site is where I put them.
